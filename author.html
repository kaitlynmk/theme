Kōhei Horikoshi is a Japanese manga artist known for creating the shōnen manga series ""Ōmagadoki Dōbutsuen", "Barrage", and most recently My Hero Academia, all of which are or were serialized in Weekly Shōnen Jump. Horikoshi is a graduate of Nagoya University of Arts and a native of Aichi Prefecture. He was a former assistant for Yasuki Tanaka, creator and author of the manga series "Hitomi no Catoblepas" and "Kagijin". Horikoshi's favorite manga series include Naruto, One Piece, Akira, Tekkonkinkreet, and Boys on the Run. Horikoshi is also an avid fan of American superhero comic books, particularly Marvel Comics.
